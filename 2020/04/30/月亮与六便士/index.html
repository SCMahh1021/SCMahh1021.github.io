Stepping on the Firm Earth, Staring at the Starry Sky
——《月亮与六便士》书评

发表于 2020-04-30 更新于 2020-05-01 分类于 文艺 阅读次数： Valine：


 Exceptionally enlightened by the The Moon And Sixpence, I am captivated by Strickland, who gives me an unprecedented feeling, which triggers me to introspect myself. On the one hand, I hold the belief that he is absolutely a scumbag for the reason that he abandons multitudinous folks who love him profoundly. Nevertheless, on the other hand, he still has numerous merits, which impress me tremendously.

Plot

 The scenario of The Moon And Sixpence is not intricate, which can be reasonably partitioned into 3 sections in accordance with circumstances and characters. Initially, Strickland relinquishes his family and identity as a middle-class stockbroker, without any explication, leaving for Paris unaccompanied, in which he suffers illness and starvation, however, still enjoying the bran-new life as a painter. Hereafter, he is assisted to rehabilitate from a life-threatening illness by Dirk Stroeve, a commercially triumphant but hackneyed Dutch painter. But it is Strickland that allures Stroeve’s wife Blanche and discards her, which leads her to cheat on her husband and suicide. Eventually, he proceeds to Tahiti, where he gets married to Ata, an indigenous girl who takes care of his daily life considerately, enjoys painting every day until his demise.

Analysis

 Provided that we say that sixpence represents the prerequisite of subsistence, you having no choice but to sink into the sea of the mundane world, then the moon is the emblem of lofty but hopeless aspiration. There is no incredulity that no one is capable of escaping from neither moon nor sixpence. As idealized as Strickland, he eventually reaches a reconciliation between the moon and sixpence before his demise. As secular as Mrs. Strickland, there is still moonbeam like amativeness spilling into her heart. The majority of us hover between both, just like what Stroeve does. We have no alternative but to proceed on the way of sixpence, nevertheless, begrudging and appreciating those who pursue their moons unhesitatingly and courageously, maintaining our cloud-castles memorable meanwhile.

Conclusion

 In our terms, how could we wrestle with the contradiction between the moon and sixpence? We struggle grievously, stive tenaciously, and decease ordinarily between both. In order to become closer to the moon, we have to squander our finite and precious energy on sixpence. But from my point of view, no matter how essential sixpence is, nothing can transform the fact that sixpence is merely the ferry ticket money to the moon.

 In conclusion, picking up six pence underfoot, looking up at the moon, stepping on the firm earth, staring at the starry sky, not sticking to the reality, not floating in ideal, this is my desired style of living.
